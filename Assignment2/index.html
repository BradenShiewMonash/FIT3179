Tourism in Australia

Introduction

Travelling, most people do it every day. While there has been a major decline in tourism due to the pandemic, the world is steadily coming around to travelling once again. Australia is no exception. This visualization aims to provide insights into the visitors to Australia, the reasons behind their travels, and how this has impacted the economy.




Recovering from the Pandemic

It is no news to anyone that the pandemic and subsequent lockdowns put a major dent in tourism. From 2020 all the way to 2022, there was practically no foreigners travelling in Australia. It was not until March 2022 when the world started being open to travelling once again. While the number of people visiting Australia is not as big as it was when it peaked in January 2020, it is rapidly returning to its former glory.




Where and Why Tourists Visit

Just what are the popular regions and why do tourists visit? From 2019 to 2024, Queensland, Victoria and New South Wales remain the top three largest points of interest. As to why people travel, the two most common reasons are for holiday, and visiting friends and reltives.




Lots to Spend

Naturally, as there are many tourists, the amount of money being spent is no short figure. What is surprising, however, is the fact that 2024 has seen the largest amount of spendings (A$47.77 billion) in the last 14 years. Perhaps this is a sign that people cannot wait to get out there and have more fun, especially after being cooped indoors for so long.




Tourism-Related Jobs

It is not all sunshine and rainbows however. In the job department, a majority of sectors have seen significant decrease when comparing 2024 to 2019. While this is to be expected, one can only hope that this will take a turn for the better for the sake of all those that have lost their jobs.




Conclusion

While the pandemic has certainly left a horrible mark in tourism, the world is steadily welcoming travelling back into its life. People are gradually warming up to returning to what was normal a few years ago. In fact, they might even exceed previous numbers.
